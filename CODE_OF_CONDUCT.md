# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all, regardless of background, identity, or experience. We pledge that participation in our community will be a harassment-free experience for everyone.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being Respectful**: Treating all members with respect and courtesy
- **Being Inclusive**: Welcoming diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- **Being Collaborative**: Working together constructively toward shared goals
- **Being Professional**: Using inclusive language and avoiding condescending behavior
- **Being Empathetic**: Considering the impact of your words and actions on others
- **Being Patient**: Understanding that people have different levels of knowledge and experience

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation based on any characteristic
- Offensive comments or personal attacks
- Trolling, spamming, or disruptive behavior
- Sharing private information without consent
- Sexual or violent content or language
- Deliberate exclusion or gatekeeping
- Any form of abuse or hate speech

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Document It**: Note specific instances, times, and context
2. **Report It**: Contact the project maintainers at [contact information]
3. **Provide Details**: Include relevant information to help investigate
4. **Confidentiality**: Reports will be handled confidentially

### Investigation

- All reports will be taken seriously and investigated fairly
- Reporters will not face retaliation
- Multiple perspectives will be considered
- Actions will be proportional to the offense

### Consequences

Violations may result in:

- **Warning**: Formal notice of unacceptable behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Removal from community for a set period
- **Permanent Ban**: Removal from community indefinitely
- **Escalation**: Reporting to appropriate authorities if necessary

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub, forums, chat, events)
- Official communications and events
- Community discussions and collaborations
- Any space representing the project or community

## Examples of Positive Behavior

### In Code Reviews

```
✅ "I noticed potential issue with this approach. Have you considered...?"
❌ "That's a stupid way to implement this."

✅ "Great work on this feature! I have a suggestion for improvement..."
❌ "This is completely wrong."
```

### In Discussions

```
✅ "I have a different perspective on this. Here's my thinking..."
❌ "Anyone who believes that is an idiot."

✅ "I'm not familiar with that approach. Can you explain it?"
❌ "That doesn't exist. You obviously don't know what you're talking about."
```

### In Community Interactions

```
✅ Welcoming new contributors and helping them get started
✅ Giving credit to others' ideas and contributions
✅ Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
✅ Acknowledging when you're wrong and learning from it
❌ Gatekeeping knowledge or being exclusive
❌ Taking credit for others' work
❌ Getting defensive about feedback
❌ Refusing to acknowledge mistakes
```

## Responsibility

Everyone in our community shares responsibility for maintaining a positive environment:

- **Leaders**: Set the example and enforce standards
- **Contributors**: Follow the Code of Conduct and report violations
- **Community**: Support each other and create an inclusive space

## Continuous Improvement

This Code of Conduct is not perfect and will be refined based on community feedback. If you have suggestions for improvement, please share them.
